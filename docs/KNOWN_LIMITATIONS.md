# Known Limitations - InsightAI Platform

This document outlines the boundaries, assumptions, and known limitations of the **InsightAI** decision intelligence platform.

---

## 1. Offline Translation & Localization Boundaries

- **Vocabulary Coverage**:
  The offline `RulesFallbackEngine` utilizes a deterministic dictionary-based translation mapping. It supports common transliterated and script-based terms in Tamil, Hindi, Tanglish, Hinglish, and Mixed. Colloquial variations or spelling mistakes not registered in the mapping dictionary will fall back to original characters, potentially degrading downstream classification accuracy.
- **Syntactic Complexity**:
  Offline translation performs word-level and phrase-level mappings rather than structural grammatical parsing. Consequently, complex sentence structures may translate into broken English phrasing, though domain-specific keywords will remain intact for taxonomy routing.

---

## 2. Voice Confidence Estimation Heuristics

- **Emotion Confidence**:
  The voice emotional index and confidence percentages are generated via MD5 text hashing combined with keyword match counts. They simulate model output distributions but are not generated by a local neural acoustic emotion model.
- **Audio Quality Score**:
  Playable WAV/MP3 duration estimates and background decibel classifications are mathematically approximated based on file size, header formats, and deterministic content hashing. This ensures consistent mock scoring metrics for demo presentations without using heavy C-level sound processing libraries.

---

## 3. Trend Forecasting Assumptions

- **Data Density**:
  The `TrendForecastingEngine` utilizes Ordinary Least Squares (OLS) linear regression models. This requires at least 3 distinct timestamps in the dataset to calculate a non-zero slope. If timestamps are missing or sparse, forecasting returns a fallback zero trend.
- **Seasonality & Volatility**:
  The forecasting model does not incorporate seasonal fluctuations, holidays, or exogenous marketing events. Projections represent short-term linear trend trajectories only.

---

## 4. Provider Failover Cascades

- **Failover Latency Overhead**:
  When a provider (e.g. OpenAI) times out or fails, the cascading failover to Gemini/Groq/Heuristics introduces a sequential latency cost equal to the timeout duration of the failed request.
- **Mock STT Routing**:
  The STT uploader falls back to a deterministic filename-matching transcription mock when internet connections are completely offline.

---

## 5. Hackathon Demo Mode Constraints

- **Static Generation Profile**:
  The 500 records generated by Demo Mode represent a shuffled, pre-configured distribution of English, Tamil, Hindi, Tanglish, and Hinglish reviews. While dates are chronologically randomized over a 30-day window, the textual feedback is selected from a finite dictionary pool.
- **Clean Ingestion Override**:
  Activating Demo Mode overrides existing database tables and active session variables.
